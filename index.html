Hanna

WORLD CLASS YOUNG ARTIST

Born in Minsk, Hanna began studying violin at the Akhremchik Gymnasium of Arts with Galina Goncharova and later moved to Saint-Petersburg where she graduated from the Special School of Music, studio of Prof. Savely Shalman, and the St. Petersburg Rimsky-Korsakov Conservatory, studio of Ilya Ioff. Hanna also completed her bachelor's degree as a Woodruff Scholar at the Schwob School of Music where she studied with Professor Sergiu Schwartz. After a year in Los Angeles, studying at UCLA with Professor Movses Pogossian and Varty Manouelian, and playing with the VEM Ensemble-in-Residence, Hanna moved to Houston, TX where she is currently pursuing her Graduate Performance Certificate in the studio of Dr. Kirsten Yon.

Career

Following her concert debut in Italy in 2009, Ms. Hrybkova has performed as a soloist and chamber musician at numerous venues in Belarus, Russia, Europe, and the United States. Hanna most recently appeared as a soloist with the UCLA Philharmonic performing on the legendary “Duke of Alcantara” Stradivarius violin from the UCLA collection, the Schwob Philharmonic, and the Georgia Philharmonic. Hanna regularly performs in different chamber ensembles and her engagements include performing with the UCLA VEM ensemble, that recently gave a West Coast concert tour and a CD recording featuring two world premieres by the Armenian composers Tigran Mansurian and Martin Ulikhanyan, which will be released in December. Other performances include collaboration with the Concertmaster of the LA Philharmonic Martin Chalifour, violinist Boris Abramov, pianist Peter Takacs, and cellist Kyung Eun Choi.

Hanna participated in masterclasses and received mentorship from the esteemed violinists Martin Chalifour, Alexander Brussilovsky, Ian Repko, Grigory Kalinovsky, Sally O’Reilly, Movses Pogossian, and Dennis Kim.

Hanna is the First Prize winner of the Atlanta Music Club competition, the Second prize Winner of the Rudolf Barshai String Competition, the Silver Medalist of the Manhattan International Music Competition, the Winner of the “All-Star” Concerto competition; the Winner of 2017-18 and 2019-20 CSU/Schwob Conservatory of Music Concerto Competitions at River Center of the Performing Arts; and the Winner of the Samuel Fordis Young Artist Concerto Competition. Hanna was also awarded the scholarship from the Glazunov Foundation and received scholarships to participate in music festivals among which are the Summer Academy in Murren, Switzerland, and the Montecito Chamber Music Festival.

Besides performing solo and chamber, she has also held positions with the Columbus Symphony Orchestra (Columbus, GA), LaGrange Symphony Orchestra (LaGrange, GA), and California Young Artist Symphony (Los Angeles, CA).
